hordriss the confuser (8 out of 10 )
One word about the design of this film... NEON! I liked the look of this film. I especially liked how they cut part of Nigma's experiment scene for the DVD release. Two face was not faithful to the original comics, but I still liked the interpretation. Chase Meridian... woah! Val Kilmer was pretty solid, although Chis O'Donnel seemed like a bit of a twerp in places. Jim Carey stole the show, with a performance heavily inspired by the 60's TV show's version of the Riddler. Just deep enough to keep your brain ticking over, with an nice subplot about Bruce Wayne deciding between being himself and Batman, which interplays nicely with the main plot throughout. The action sequences and stunts were impressive without resorting to complete lunacy (MI2, anyone?). One final note: Where does Two Face hire all his goons? Can you imagine the advert "Wanted: large men with mediocre fighting ability to wield fluorescent machine guns and operate assorted vehicles. Ludicrous piercings and criminal record essential. Cover for medical, dental, and psychiatric care not included. Proof of life insurance required". I don't think it's fair to compare either The Dark Knight or Batman Begins with Batman Forever. They are on entirely different wavelengths. I rate this second only to the first Tim Burton version (we shall not speak of the 60's film based on the TV series, hehe).
